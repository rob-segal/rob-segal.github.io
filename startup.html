Background: Exoskeletons for Rehabilitation

I initially collaborated on refinements of a hand rehabilitation exoskeleton, aiming to assist patients in regaining mobility.
However, adoption in hospitals was slow, leading us to explore new applications for exoskeleton technology.
This pivot sparked the idea of bringing exoskeletons into virtual reality. Exoflex

Identifying the Virtual Reality Problem

With limited funding to immediately develop exoskeleton-based VR solutions, we turned our attention to pressing challenges in the VR industry.
Motion sickness emerged as a major issue, caused by the disconnect between virtual movement and physical stillness.
We saw an opportunity to create a hardware solution that could bridge this gap.
